#first blog!: visiting companies

06/27/16

Yay guys! First post! Welcome to my blogs which are basically going to be like my digital diaries! They'll range from traveling adventures to food experiences to everything in between. So without further ado, let me present you my first blog post on visiting cool ass companies!

On June 23rd, I got to visit two major companies in the tech business: Yelp and Github. It was an incredible experience, to say the least. I encourage everyone to try to get a tour around the companies because I got amazing advice from amazing people. Huge thanks to Quinn Slack from Sourcegraph for giving us the idea & Zach from Hack Camp for helping me write it!

First stop of the day: Yelp!

Isn't the building beautiful? It used to be a old Pacific Bell Building but now it is occupied by many businesses, Yelp being one of them.

The building is also super tall (I had to really bend my 5'2 self to take this picture.)

Anyways, onwards! We had to pass through front desk security, and then traveled up up up to the lobby! The lobby is very cool, as expected.

Yelp's Lobby

I met with Jenni Snyder, the Engineering Manager at Yelp and one of the co-leaders of their Awesome Women in Engineering (AWE) group, and she showed us several floors of the building. All of these floors showed dedicated people hard at work and it really inspired me to work hard as well. On Jenni's floor, we met with another member of AWE: Maria. Maria uses algorithms to make the predictions in Yelp possible for the users.

After meeting up with Maria, we all went to a floor which was completely a chill zone; it had a coffeeshop, plenty of open couches and chairs, and even a 80s-style light-up wall decal.

Latte from Yelp

We began discussing about their daily life at work, how they got there, and other interesting stuff. Jenni and Maria even got to see our projects we made at Hack Camp! It was such a fun experience at Yelp and I wish I could do this all the time.

Next stop: Sushiritto!

I have waited very long for this day. I only got to stare longlingly at it in pictures, hold in my hunger and desire, and imagine the day that I will go as well... And I finally did! I got to finally try Sushiritto.

Jiabei, my friend who went with me, and I searched for Sushiritto, which my Yelp app informed was only 0.2 miles away from the building and should only take 2 minutes. Funny thing, we got lost and it took us almost 10 minutes to find the famous sushi place. There was already a huge line snaking out from the tiny shop, but the wait was worth. At first, I had thought Sushiritto was customizable like Chipotle but it turned out to be already pre-designed. I chose the Sumo Crunch, which had tempura, crab meat, and their "special" green guac all rolled up with a crunchy red crumbs shell.

Sumo Crunch from Sushiritto! Super filling & good!

Final stop: Github!

Last stop of the day was Github, where all of our projects are stored! We met with Natalie and another lady who were on the marketing team and took care of the Github shop. Github was generous in letting us collect unlimited stickers from their sticker bowls and giving us a free T-shirt as well!
